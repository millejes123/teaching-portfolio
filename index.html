Introduction
About Me

I love teaching English, building creative projects, and helping students from all cultures reach their goals. There is nothing more rewarding than watching a student find their voice in a new language, connect with a piece of history, or surprise themselves with what they're capable of. I show up every day excited to design experiences that make that happen.

At Austin ISD International High School, I teach Ethnic Studies and Geography to emergent bilingual students — adapting grade-level content to meet students where they are in their English journey while keeping expectations high for every learner.

Before returning to the U.S., I spent nearly six years in South Korea building and growing ESL programs at schools and startups like AMJ Studios and Spoon English, where I helped shape their early English curriculum. I care deeply about equity, inclusive classrooms, and the power of creative teaching to open doors for students who are still finding their voice in English.

In the Classroom
Teaching Portfolio
🌐
Model UN Simulations
Critical Thinking

Students represent countries in the UN by researching a global issue, presenting their country's position in English, and then voting on a resolution after everyone has presented. This activity builds academic vocabulary, persuasive speaking, and global citizenship — perfect for emergent bilinguals who need authentic, high-stakes practice.

🎤
Harvard Case Method Discussions
Discussion-Based

Inspired by real-world case studies, I use this method to get students to analyze issues in Ethnic Studies by thinking about what it would be like to have to make a decision in a historical figure's shoes. For example, students examine Colin Kaepernick's decision to kneel during the national anthem — weighing the risks, values, and context he faced — before discussing and debating what they would have done.

🎬
Student YouTube Video Projects
Creative Media

Students script, film, and edit their own English-language videos — documenting their learning journey for parents and showcasing growth. I directed 20 of these projects at AMJ Studios, building student confidence and giving families a tangible window into progress.

Culturally Responsive Research Projects
Culturally Responsive

Students explore history and culture through a variety of research methods — oral history interviews with family members, case study analysis, and content analysis using films and media. These projects validate students' lived experiences as academic knowledge while building literacy and critical thinking skills in a culturally affirming way.

🦸
AI Superhero Capstone Project
AI + Ethnic Studies

For the end-of-year Ethnic Studies capstone, students use Google Gemini and Microsoft Copilot to create their own superhero from their home culture. After studying stereotypes and why representation in media matters, students pitch an original movie script featuring a superhero who breaks stereotypes and reflects their cultural identity — bringing together research, creative writing, and AI tools in one powerful project.

Engagement

At Spoon English, I designed workshops where adult learners practiced English through shared interests — calligraphy, baking, film discussions, and more. Lowering the stakes made the language feel accessible, and students left with both new skills and new confidence.
